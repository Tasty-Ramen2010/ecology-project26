Chapter 01 · The City You Know

Cairo is a city of sun and stone — built on a desert, cradled by the Nile, and held in place by winds that have blown west-to-east for ten thousand years.

Today, Cairo sits at 30° north, squarely inside a band of dry subtropical air. Westerly winds roll in over North Africa carrying almost no moisture. The Mokattam Plateau to the east forces any stray air mass to descend, compress, and warm — a classic rain-shadow, and the reason the nearby desert is so extreme.

Seasons here come in two registers: a mild winter that hovers between 50 and 75°F, and a long, blunt summer that routinely breaks 95°F. Water is the Nile and the Nile alone — a green ribbon threading through an ochre world, feeding the canals, Lake Qarun, and the Wadi El Natrun basins on its way to the sea.
